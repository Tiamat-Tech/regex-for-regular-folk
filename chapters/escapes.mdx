## Escapes

In regex, some characters have special meanings as we have explored in the last few chapters:

-   `|`
-   `{`, `}`
-   `(`, `)`
-   `[`, `]`
-   `^`, `$`
-   `+`, `*`, `?`
-   `\`
-   `.`

    Literal only within character classes.

-   `-`

    _Sometimes_ a special character within character classes. Literal otherwise.

---

When we wish to match these character _literally_, we need to "escape" them.

This is done by **prefixing the character with a `\`**.

---

<Example source="\(paren\)" flags="g">
    <String>paren</String>
    <String>(paren)</String>
    <String>a (paren)</String>
</Example>
<Example source="(paren)" flags="g">
    <String>paren</String>
    <String>parents</String>
    <String>(paren)</String>
    <String>a (paren)</String>
</Example>

---

<Example source="example\.com" flags="g">
    <String>example.com</String>
    <String>a.example.com/foo</String>
    <String>example_com</String>
    <String>example@com</String>
    <String>example_com/foo</String>
</Example>
<Example source="example.com" flags="g">
    <String>example.com</String>
    <String>a.example.com/foo</String>
    <String>example_com</String>
    <String>example@com</String>
    <String>example_com/foo</String>
</Example>

---

<Example source="A\+" flags="g">
    <String>A+</String>
    <String>A+B</String>
    <String>5A+</String>
    <String>AAA</String>
</Example>
<Example source="A+" flags="g">
    <String>A+</String>
    <String>A+B</String>
    <String>5A+</String>
    <String>AAA</String>
</Example>

---

<Example source="worth \$5" flags="g">
    <String>worth $5</String>
    <String>worth $54</String>
    <String>not worth $5</String>
</Example>
<Example source="worth $5" flags="g">
    <String>worth $5</String>
    <String>worth $54</String>
    <String>not worth $5</String>
</Example>
