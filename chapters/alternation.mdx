---
title: Alternation
---

Alternation allows matching _one_ of several phrases. This is more powerful than character classes, which allow matching one of several characters.

Delimit the set of phrases with pipes—`|`.

<Example regex={/foo|bar|baz/g}>
    <li>foo baz</li>
    <li>Your food</li>
    <li>Behind bars</li>
</Example>

> One of `foo`, `bar`, and `baz`

---

If only a part of the regex is to be "alternated", wrap that part with a [group](/chapter/groups)—capturing or non-capturing.

<Example regex={/Try (foo|bar|baz)/g}>
    <li>Try foo</li>
    <li>Try bar</li>
    <li>Try baz</li>
    <li>Try food</li>
</Example>

> `Try` followed by one of `foo`, `bar`, and `baz`

---

Matching numbers between 100 and 250:

<Example regex={/1\d\d|2[0-4]\d|250/g}>
    <li>100, 157, 199</li>
    <li>139 + 140 = 279</li>
    <li>201 INR</li>
    <li>$220</li>
    <li>250</li>
    <li>1250</li>
    <li>e = 2.71828182...</li>
    <li>251</li>
    <li>729</li>
</Example>

## Examples

### IP Addresses

<Example
    regex={
        /\b(?:(?:2(?:[0-4][0-9]|5[0-5])|[0-1]?[0-9]?[0-9])\.){3}(?:(?:2([0-4][0-9]|5[0-5])|[0-1]?[0-9]?[0-9]))\b/g
    }
>
    <li>9.9.9.9</li>
    <li>127.0.0.1:8080</li>
    <li>It's 192.168.1.9</li>
    <li>255.193.09.243</li>
    <li>123.123.123.123</li>
    <li>123.123.123.256</li>
    <li>0.0.x.0</li>
</Example>

### Roman numerals

<Example regex={/^M{0,4}(CM|CD|D?C{0,3})(XC|XL|L?X{0,3})(IX|IV|V?I{0,3})$/g}>
    <li>MMXX</li>
    <li>VI</li>
    <li>XX</li>
    <li>XI</li>
    <li>IXI</li>
    <li>VV</li>
</Example>
